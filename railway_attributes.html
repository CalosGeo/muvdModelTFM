Railway Attribute value concepts

En esta página encontrará las tablas que corresponden con los atributos que puede editar de la entidad Railway. Cada tabla muestra los diferentes valores que puede tomar ese atributo y la explicación de los mismos.

Atributos

(PCF) Physical Condition

Name Code Definition Description
construction1The man-made structure is being built or undergoing renovation or repair.Incorporates site preparations, building of new construction and the renovation or repair of existing structures.
damaged4The man-made structure is damaged.Damage may be a consequence of accident, military action, or natural disaster. The damaged man-made structure can be repaired.
destroyed6The man-made structure has undergone complete destruction and is in a state of total disrepair.Destruction may be a consequence of military action, natural disaster, planned demolition or a long period of no maintenance. A destroyed man-made structure can only be restored by complete reconstruction.
dismantled5The man-made structure has undergone a systematic selective removal of components.Low value components and/or equipment may remain. The man-made structure cannot be repaired without a great deal of time and effort.
intact2The man-made structure is complete and intact.The man-made structure may or may not be in operation or use.
unmaintained3The man-made structure is left in place to deteriorate.The man-made structure has been abandoned and no maintenance or repairs are being carried out. While the structure is physically intact, no signs of operation are visible.
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

(RRA) Railway Power Method

Name Code Definition Description
electrifiedTrack1Electrical power is transferred using an electrified third rail.
nonElectrified4Electrical power is not provided by the railway.On-board electrical generators or batteries may be used.
overheadElectrified3Electrical power is transferred from an overhead power line.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

(RRC) Railway Use

Name Code Definition Description
automatedTransitSystem32An automated rail or guideway system that runs along dedicated ways.May operate according to a fixed schedule or on-demand (sometimes capable of bypassing individual stations). Generally consisting of vehicles having capacities of 12 to 100 people. Examples of this type of system include the Personal Rapid Transit (PRT) at West Virginia University and airport people-movers in U.S. cities such as Seattle, Dallas-Ft. Worth and Miami.
carline2A track for streetcars, trolleys, and other mass transit rail systems.
craneMovement34Designed to provide mobility for a travelling crane or a moveable gantry.Often aligned parallel to the face of a wharf in order that the crane may be used to load and unload cargo vessels.
funicular15A railway designed to operate on steep grades.Usually includes mechanical components (for example: a rack) to insure traction at all times.
industrial37A railway used for the transportation of materials and/or cargo within a manufacturing, processing, or resource extraction facility.These rail lines will not be connected to any national or regional rail networks.
logging8A railway designed to efficiently transport logs from remote logging sites.Usually narrow gauge and more curved than railways intended for high-speed transport.
longHaul33A railway operated over long distances and typically passing without interruption through urban areas.
marineRailway13A slipway in which the cradle travels on rails to facilitate the handling of vessels.
marineVesselTow36A railway, aligned parallel to the edge of a body of water, used to tow marine vessels (ex. boats, barges, ships).
museum24Maintained as an historical exhibit at a railway museum.
railRapidTransit11A rail-based high-speed public transport system.Usually located within a metropolitan area.
spaceVehicleTransportation35A railway used to transport space vehicles between the launch pad and a preparation facility (i.e. hangar or assembly building).
tramway14A railway laid down on a public road or street for tramcars.
undergroundRailway6A railway running underground, especially beneath the streets and buildings of a city.Usually includes those portions of an underground railway system that may not be underground. Common names for underground railways include: subway, metro, or underground (ex. New York Subway, Paris Metro, and London Underground).
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

multipleArrangements3Three or more linear features on the same support structure.
singleArrangement1One linear feature.

(RWC) Railway Class

Name Code Definition Description
branchLine3A secondary railway line running from a main line to a terminus.For example, a railhead.
highSpeedRail1A rail-based high-speed inter-city transport system.Usually operated over long distances. For example, the French TGV (Train Ó Grande Vitesse).
mainLine2A chief railway line operated over long distances and typically passing without interruption through multiple urban areas.Usually must not be occupied or traversed without proper authority. Primarily carries freight and is accordingly operated at a relatively slow speed.

(TRT) Track Type

Name Code Definition Description
drillTrack2Connects to a ladder track, over which locomotives and rail wagons move back and forth in switching.
houseTrack3Enters, or is located along side, a freight house.Rail wagons are moved to a house track for loading or unloading.
jointTrack4Owned, operated, or maintained by more than one operating railway company.
ladderTrack5A series of turnouts providing access to any of several parallel tracks in a railway yard.
maglev13A railway using magnetic repulsion to support the train above the track.May also be used in an overhead monorail.
monorail12A railway in which the track is a single rail, on or suspended from which vehicles run.
pairedTrack6A pair of adjacent tracks where when two railway companies own single railway line and they have reached an agreement whereby one railway company services one railway track and the other company services the other railway track.
ripTrack7A small repair facility for rail wagons consisting of a single railway track in a small railway yard.The name is derived from 'Repair, Inspect and Paint'.
stubTrack9A form of a side track connected to a running track at one end only, and protected at the other end by an obstruction (for example: a bumping post).
teamTrack10A track on which rail wagons are placed for the use of the public in loading or unloading freight.
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
