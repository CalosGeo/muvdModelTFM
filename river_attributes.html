River Attribute value concepts

En esta página encontrará las tablas que corresponden con los atributos que puede editar de la entidad River. Cada tabla muestra los diferentes valores que puede tomar ese atributo y la explicación de los mismos.

Atributos

(HYP) Hydrologic Persistence

Name Code Definition Description
dry4Filled and/or flowing infrequently, generally only during and/or immediately after heavy precipitation.The waterbody is often vegetated (for example: with shrubs); such a streambed in the Southwestern United States is termed a 'derramadero'.
intermittent2Filled and/or flowing for part of the year.
perennial1Filled and/or flowing continuously throughout the year as its bed lies below the water table.
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

(NVS) Navigability Information

Name Code Definition Description
navigable3Affording passage to vessels; capable of being navigated.
navigableAndOperational1Affords passage to vessels and is operating in accordance with stated schedule and/or stated depth and clearance.For example, a channel is maintained at stated depth or a lock is manned on a regular schedule.
navigableButAbandoned2Affords passage to vessels but operational support has been abandoned.For example, a channel remains navigable but may no longer be maintained at stated depth.
navigablePeriodicRestrict4Affords passage to vessels but periodic restrictions apply.For example, seasonally restricted due to ice or only manned at regular intervals.
notNavigable5Does not afford passage to vessels; incapable of being navigated.
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

(SCC) Water Type

Name Code Definition Description
alkaline1Water with a pH above 7.0.
brackish12Water containing dissolved salts and minerals greater than 1,000 to less than or equal to 15,000 milligrams per litre.Brackish water may result from mixing of seawater with fresh water, as in estuaries, or it may occur naturally, as in brackish fossil aquifers.
brine14Water containing greater than 40,000 milligrams per litre of total dissolved solids (TDS).Saltier than full strength seawater.
fresh11Containing less than 1,000 milligrams per litre of dissolved salts or minerals.Chloride is less than or equal to 600 milligrams per litre and sulphates are less than or equal to 300 milligrams per litre.
mineral4Water which has natural mineral salts or gases (carbon dioxide) and contains at least 250 milligrams per litre of dissolved salts and minerals.
saline10Water containing greater than 15,000 to less than or equal 30,000 milligrams per litre of total dissolved solids (TDS).
seawater13Water containing greater than 30,000 to less than or equal 40,000 milligrams per litre of total dissolved solids (TDS).
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

(WCC) Watercourse Channel Type

Name Code Definition Description
braidedStream2A watercourse divided into an intricate network of interlacing channels.Generally shallow, the smaller channels are continually shifting and are separated by bar deposits.
channelizedStream1A permanent or relocated watercourse that has been diverted, dredged, straightened and/or dyked.
gorge3A deep, narrow, V-shaped channel with steep rocky sides.
normalChannel7A watercourse that has not been channelized or relocated.Follows a normal meandering channel with variations in channel bed morphology.
wadi4A dry, intermittent, or ephemeral drainage course marked by deposits of alluvial material that are not confined to a specific channel.The channels are commonly shallow and braided, can cover a wide area being nearly level with the surrounding floodplain, and are separated by flat, low lying segments.
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.
No Information-999999There is no information specified regarding the attribute value.

(WST) Watercourse Sink Type

Name Code Definition Description
disappearing2Disappears suddenly into the ground and partially or completely ceases flowing on the surface.
dissipating1Gradually dissipates due to a lowering of the water table.
Other999The attribute value is known, but is not currently a valid member of the attribute range.The actual value may have been previously, or may become in the future, a valid member of the attribute range.

(YWQ) Water Potability

Name Code Definition Description
contaminated3Incapable of being easily treated to become potable.For example, requiring distillation or reverse osmosis treatment.
nonpotable4Unsuitable for, or not intended for, consumption by humans.
potable1Suitable for consumption by humans.
treatable2Capable of being relatively easily treated to become potable.For example, treatable using simple filtration and/or disinfection.
